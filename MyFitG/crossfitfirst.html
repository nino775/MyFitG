First Time Doing CrossFit Guide

CrossFit can be intense, but here’s a guide to help you get started safely and make the most out of your first session!

Watch: CrossFit for Beginners

CrossFit 101 - Introduction and Tips for Beginners

What to Do

  • Warm Up Properly: CrossFit workouts can be intense, so start with a proper warm-up to prepare your muscles and joints.
  • Focus on Technique: Begin with lighter weights and master proper form and technique to avoid injury.
  • Follow a Scaled Workout: Many CrossFit exercises can be scaled down to suit beginners. Don’t be afraid to modify movements.
  • Stay Hydrated: CrossFit workouts are high-intensity, so be sure to drink water before, during, and after your session.
  • Listen to Your Coach: Coaches provide valuable feedback on form and modifications. Pay attention to their advice.
  • Cool Down and Stretch: Finish with a cool-down and stretching to help your body recover and reduce soreness.

What Not to Do

  • Don’t Overdo It: CrossFit is intense. Start slowly, and don’t push yourself to exhaustion on your first day.
  • Don’t Skip the Warm-Up: Jumping into high-intensity exercises without warming up increases the risk of injury.
  • Avoid Competing with Others: Focus on your own progress and avoid comparing yourself to experienced CrossFitters.
  • Don’t Ignore Pain: If you feel sharp or unusual pain, stop immediately. There’s a difference between discomfort and injury.
  • Don’t Sacrifice Form for Speed: CrossFit often involves timed workouts, but always prioritize good form over speed.
  • Don’t Forget Recovery: Allow your body to rest and recover, especially after an intense CrossFit session.
